## Plain-language definition
The superior temporal gyrus is a lateral temporal cortical region commonly associated with auditory processing and speech-related perception. In human neuroimaging and lesion studies, STG activity is frequently observed during listening, phonological analysis, and parts of language comprehension, but these functions are distributed across broader temporal-frontal networks rather than localized to one “center.”

## Associated with (not deterministic)
- Auditory and speech-sound processing in task fMRI — **Evidence:** established [Hickok2007]
- Early/intermediate language-comprehension pathways (network-level) — **Evidence:** established [Hickok2007]
- Clinical language deficits when temporal perisylvian networks are disrupted — **Evidence:** emerging [Dronkers2004]

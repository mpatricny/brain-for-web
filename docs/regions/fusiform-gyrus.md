## Plain-language definition
The fusiform gyrus is a ventral temporal cortical region associated with high-level visual recognition, including faces and complex object categories. Evidence supports robust involvement in visual expertise and face-related processing, while exact functional specialization depends on subregion, task, and network interactions with occipital and temporal areas.

## Associated with (not deterministic)
- Face-selective responses in ventral temporal cortex (FFA-related territory) — **Evidence:** established [Kanwisher1997]
- High-level visual recognition and category expertise effects — **Evidence:** established [Haxby2001]
- Clinical face-recognition impairment when ventral occipitotemporal networks are damaged — **Evidence:** emerging [Barton2008]

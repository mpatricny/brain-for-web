## Plain-language definition
The precentral gyrus contains cortical territory strongly associated with voluntary motor control and movement execution. Human stimulation, lesion, and functional imaging studies consistently implicate this area in motor output and motor planning interfaces, but behavior arises from coordinated motor networks including premotor, parietal, basal ganglia, cerebellar, and spinal systems.

## Associated with (not deterministic)
- Voluntary movement execution and corticospinal output — **Evidence:** established [Penfield1937]
- Task-related motor activation and somatotopic organization — **Evidence:** established [Sanes2000]
- Motor learning/plasticity in interaction with broader motor circuits — **Evidence:** emerging [Dayan2011]
